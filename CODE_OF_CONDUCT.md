# CornerFloat Community Code of Conduct

CornerFloat should be a place where people can learn, contribute, disagree, and
ship careful work without being intimidated or demeaned.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent while still addressing concrete harm.
- Welcome contributors with different experience levels and backgrounds.
- Protect private information seen in screenshots, logs, crash reports, and
  authentication flows.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope while remaining free to propose a
  different direction constructively.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, deliberate
misgendering, doxxing, sustained disruption, and publishing another person's
private information are not tolerated. Security reports or logs must never be
used to expose credentials, account data, or identifiable browsing activity.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers through the repository's
private contact channel. Do not open a public issue if the report contains
private or identifying information. Maintainers will review the available facts,
limit disclosure to people who need to respond, and may edit or remove content,
lock discussions, reject contributions, or temporarily or permanently restrict
participation.

Anyone handling a report must disclose conflicts of interest and step aside when
appropriate. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation
is itself a violation.

## Scope

This code applies in the repository, issue tracker, review discussions, project
community spaces, and public interactions where someone is representing
CornerFloat.
