Privacy Policy

CornerFloat · Effective July 22, 2026

Short version: CornerFloat does not operate an account service, analytics service, or advertising service. Its settings and workspace library stay on your Mac. Websites you open remain governed by those websites’ own privacy policies.

Data CornerFloat stores

CornerFloat stores preferences, user-defined Quick Sites (names, aliases, and destination URLs), favorites, recent destinations, and saved workspace layouts locally in your macOS user account. Web sign-in sessions, cookies, and website data are stored by Apple WebKit’s persistent website data store. Downloads are saved only to a location you select.

Settings can export the CornerFloat library as versioned JSON. The export contains those library collections only; it does not contain website cookies, passwords, account sessions, downloads, or WebKit website data. Import validates and previews the complete file before asking to replace the current library.

Permissions

  • Ordinary web panels do not themselves need a special macOS privacy permission.
  • When an HTTPS website requests the microphone—normally after you start voice or dictation—CornerFloat allows WebKit to present a decision. macOS permission for CornerFloat and WebKit permission for that website are separate, user-controlled choices; neither is granted automatically.
  • Camera requests, including combined camera-and-microphone requests, are denied.
  • Launch at Login is registered with the macOS Login Items service only after you change that switch in Settings.
  • A local source build hides the release-only Passkey action. In an eligible signed release, Passkey access is requested only after you choose Enable or Review Passkey Access…; it is never requested at launch. macOS controls whether the browser may use passkeys from Apple Passwords or compatible credential managers.

Network requests

Network traffic comes from websites you choose to open and, when automatic updates are enabled, from the configured CornerFloat update feed. A feed request may include standard HTTP information such as app version, macOS version, IP address, and user agent. CornerFloat adds no cross-site tracking or advertising identifiers.

Third-party websites

Google, Microsoft, ChatGPT, and other sites may collect information under their own terms. CornerFloat does not control those services. OAuth and passkey prompts are handled by macOS, AuthenticationServices, WebKit, and the corresponding identity provider. CornerFloat does not copy passkeys into its own library.

CornerFloat does not itself record, store, or upload microphone audio. After you approve both microphone permissions, audio is captured for and handled by the HTTPS website you selected under that website’s terms and privacy policy. That website may transmit or retain audio independently of CornerFloat.

Bluetooth microphone routing

Before WebKit presents a website microphone decision on a Bluetooth input/output route that may reduce voice quality, CornerFloat reads the current local audio-device and route information needed for that risk check and displays a native choice. It does not persist that information. When a built-in input is available, Use Mac Microphone changes the system default input only after you click it; Continue with Bluetooth leaves the route unchanged; Cancel denies that request. Without a built-in alternative, the first option is omitted. The separate macOS and website permissions remain under your control.

A temporary input change is system-wide and may be visible to other audio apps. CornerFloat attempts to restore the previous input when website capture ends, the panel closes, or the app quits, but only while the default input still matches the device CornerFloat selected. It relinquishes restoration ownership when it observes a later default-input change. CornerFloat does not copy, analyze, persist, or transmit microphone audio to a CornerFloat service.

Deleting local data

You can clear recent destinations from Library and export a backup from Settings → Local Data. To remove Quick Sites, favorites, recents, and saved workspaces, quit CornerFloat and delete ~/Library/Application Support/CornerFloat. Preferences are stored separately by macOS; while CornerFloat is quit, run defaults delete com.calvinkai.cornerfloat to reset them. WebKit cookies and website data are also separate and must be removed from the app’s WebKit data in your macOS user Library if you want to clear website sessions.

Changes and support

The effective date above will change when this policy materially changes. Open CornerFloat → Support for troubleshooting, diagnostics, and data-removal instructions.
